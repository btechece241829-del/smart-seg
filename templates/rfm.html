RFM Metrics - Behavioural Customer Segmentation

Recency · Frequency · Monetary - evaluating customer value from past transaction history

What is RFM?

RFM metrics are a core component of behavioural customer segmentation, allowing businesses to evaluate a customer's value based on their past transaction history. Each dimension captures a critical signal, and together they form a complete behavioural fingerprint that drives personalised marketing decisions.

R
R
Recency
Measures the time elapsed since a customer's last purchase. Customers who bought recently are more likely to respond to new promotions and are considered more active.
Mall Dataset Proxy - Spending Score (1-100) A high spending score reflects an actively engaged, recently purchasing customer. Score >= 60 = High R. Score 35-59 = Moderate R. Score < 35 = Low R.
F
F
Frequency
Tracks how often a customer completes a transaction within a period. This helps identify loyal users who shop habitually versus one-time buyers.
Mall Dataset Proxy - Age Bracket Younger customers (Age <= 30) statistically visit malls and complete transactions more frequently. Age <= 30 = High F. Age 31-45 = Moderate F. Age > 45 = Low F.
M
M
Monetary
Calculates the total revenue generated by the customer. This represents their financial contribution and helps prioritise high-value customers.
Mall Dataset Proxy - Annual Income (k INR) Higher income directly correlates with higher spending capacity and monetary contribution. Income >= ₹70k = High M. ₹45-69k = Moderate M. < ₹45k = Low M.
